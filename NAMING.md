**Winner: Agent B — Hearth**

The committee accepted Agent B's name and subtitle direction with one modification: "AI Finance for Two" was refined to "AI Finance for Couples" to surface the highest-intent search keyword in the Finance category ("Couples") without changing the rhythm, tone, or brand direction.

**Committee Rationale:**

Hearth is the clear winner by a meaningful margin over Nudge (41 vs 35 total points). The decision rests on four interlocking strengths:

1. **Emotional fit for the target segment.** Millennial and Gen Z couples managing shared money are experiencing one of the highest-stress relationship dynamics there is — a name that evokes warmth, home, and gathering is precisely the right emotional register. "Nudge" is clever but cerebral; "Hearth" is felt.

2. **Brand scalability.** Hearth can grow into homeownership tracking, family budgeting, insurance, and household financial planning without the name ever feeling wrong. "Nudge" is locked to the behavioral-coaching concept and would feel strained if the product expanded.

3. **Word-of-mouth mechanics.** "Hearth" is a confident, complete proper noun that sounds like a brand the moment someone hears it. It contains "heart" phonetically without being saccharine about it. "Nudge" as a category noun risks feeling generic or easily confused with productivity and wellness apps using the same coaching metaphor.

4. **ASO with the modified subtitle.** "AI Finance for Couples" (22 chars) stacks three of the highest-intent search terms in the Finance category — AI, Finance, Couples — while keeping the brand name clean.

## Proposals

### Agent A — Hearth

**Subtitle:** Budget Together, Grow Together
**Tagline:** Where couples build financial futures, side by side.

**Rationale:**

Hearth wins on multiple fronts simultaneously. The word is a universally understood symbol of home, warmth, and shared life — the place where a household gathers. This emotional anchor is perfect for couples managing finances together: it signals safety, partnership, and domestic stability without feeling stuffy or corporate. Unlike abstract fintech names (Copilot, Monarch) or overly literal ones (Honeydue), Hearth is a single evocative word that communicates the app's couples-first identity without spelling it out. It is pronounceable, spell-obvious, and deeply shareable in conversation ("we use Hearth for our finances"). From an ASO standpoint, the word "hearth" is not claimed by any major finance app, giving Hearth a clean path to own its brand namespace. The word also naturally surfaces when users search "household finance app" and "home budget app" — two high-intent queries. The name scales with the product: Hearth can expand into homeownership tracking, shared investment goals, or family budgeting without the name feeling dated. It is the kind of name that earns trust on first hearing.

**Brand Analysis:**

- Memorability Score: 9
- Uniqueness Score: 8
- Emotional Resonance: Warm, safe, and grounded — evokes the image of a home and two people building a life together. Avoids the cold, transactional tone that makes most finance apps feel like chores. Couples will associate the name with calm and partnership rather than stress and spreadsheets.
- Potential Confusion With: None identified.

**Weakness:**

The word "hearth" has a slightly archaic, literary quality that skews toward older millennials. Some Gen Z users may not immediately associate it with a fireplace or home, requiring a brief orientation period. It also does not contain an obvious finance keyword in the name itself, meaning the app must rely on its subtitle and keyword field to signal the finance category to the App Store algorithm — a manageable but real constraint.

**Icon Concept:**

A stylized flame or arch shape — the classic hearth silhouette — rendered in a warm terracotta or deep amber against a clean off-white or warm cream background. The icon should feel iOS-native: rounded corners, soft gradient on the flame, no drop shadows. Optionally, two intertwining curves form the arch, subtly nodding to partnership. The overall aesthetic matches Copilot-level polish: minimal, premium, instantly readable at small sizes on the Home Screen.

### Agent B — Hearth (Selected Winner)

**Subtitle (original):** AI Finance for Two
**Subtitle (adopted, modified by committee):** AI Finance for Couples
**Tagline:** Where your money comes home.

**Rationale:**

Hearth wins on every dimension simultaneously. First, the metaphor is perfect: a hearth is the literal center of a home — warm, shared, the place where a household gathers. That maps directly onto the product's core promise of a unified household financial view. Second, it signals calm and trust rather than anxiety, which is exactly what couples managing shared money need. Unlike Honeydue (which leans cutesy) or Monarch (which leans corporate-authoritative), Hearth feels intimate and modern without being twee. Third, it is one clean, confident syllable — impossible to misspell, easy to say out loud ("have you checked Hearth?"), and it sounds like a proper noun brand immediately. Fourth, the word contains "heart" phonetically, giving it a subtle emotional layer about care and partnership without being heavy-handed about it. Fifth, it is genuinely differentiated in the Finance category — no major app currently holds this name, and it does not sound like a generic bank or budgeting tool. Sixth, the name scales: Hearth can expand into a broader household financial OS (insurance, mortgages, family planning) without the name feeling wrong. The AI coaching angle fits naturally — a hearth that keeps the home from going cold is a perfect metaphor for proactive spending intervention.

**Brand Analysis:**

- Memorability Score: 9
- Uniqueness Score: 8
- Emotional Resonance: Warm, safe, and grounded — evokes the feeling of a stable home and partnership without any financial anxiety. The hearth as a gathering place mirrors the app's promise of bringing both partners' finances into one shared view.
- Potential Confusion With: Hearth is a home services / HVAC company in the US (Hearth.com, financing for home improvement). There is minor namespace collision risk but it operates in an entirely different category and target audience. On the App Store Finance category specifically, the name is clean.

**Weakness:**

The biggest risk is the Hearth.com brand collision in the home improvement financing space. While the App Store Finance category is clean, PR coverage or app review articles might surface the confusion. Additionally, "Hearth" does not contain an obvious finance keyword, meaning ASO keyword density must live entirely in the subtitle and keyword field — the name itself contributes zero search signal for terms like "budget" or "money". The warmth metaphor also skews slightly domestic/traditional, which could feel less cutting-edge to the tech-forward segment of the Gen Z target audience who might prefer something more futuristic.

**Icon Concept:**

A minimal, warm icon: a stylized flame or ember shape rendered as a single clean geometric form — not a literal fire, but an abstracted teardrop or upward-pointing rounded triangle suggesting warmth and home. Deep terracotta or burnt sienna as the primary color against a warm off-white or cream background. The shape doubles visually as both a flame and a house roofline if viewed abstractly. No text on the icon. The overall feel is premium iOS-native — think Copilot's restraint but warmer in palette. Could also work as a solid warm coral on deep charcoal for a dark-mode-first aesthetic.

### Agent C — Nudge

**Subtitle:** Budget & Finance for Couples
**Tagline:** Spend smarter together — before it's too late.

**Rationale:**

Nudge is a single, punchy, phonetically clean word that directly names the app's core differentiator: proactive AI intervention BEFORE overspending happens. The word "nudge" is already embedded in behavioral economics language (Thaler and Sunstein's nudge theory), so it carries implicit intelligence and intentionality without feeling corporate. It signals "gentle guidance" rather than "alarm or punishment," which is exactly the emotional register couples need around shared money conversations — calm, collaborative, not accusatory. The name is short enough to be instantly memorable in word-of-mouth ("have you tried Nudge?"), easy to spell and search, and visually simple for an icon treatment. The subtitle does the heavy ASO lifting by front-loading "Budget" and "Finance" — two of the top-searched Finance category terms — and the word "Couples" immediately filters the right audience and differentiates from solo-finance apps like Copilot. The combination of a single-word brand name with a keyword-dense subtitle mirrors the ASO strategy used by breakout finance apps (Copilot, Monarch) that keep the brand clean while letting the subtitle carry search weight.

**Brand Analysis:**

- Memorability Score: 9
- Uniqueness Score: 8
- Emotional Resonance: Warm, non-judgmental, and action-oriented. "Nudge" feels like a gentle tap on the shoulder from a trusted friend rather than a financial alarm — this reduces the anxiety couples feel around shared money and positions the AI coach as supportive rather than intrusive.
- Potential Confusion With: There is a UK financial platform called NudgeGlobal (B2B employee benefits), and a few minor productivity apps use "Nudge" branding. However, none are consumer iOS finance apps in the US market. The App Store currently has no dominant finance app with this exact name, making it acquirable and defensible.

**Weakness:**

The word "nudge" does not contain a high-value finance keyword in the app name itself, which means the name alone will not rank for "budget" or "finance" searches — it depends entirely on the subtitle and metadata to carry keyword weight. Additionally, "nudge" as a concept may be too abstract for users who want an immediately descriptive name that tells them what the app does on first encounter. Early paid acquisition costs may be higher until brand recognition builds, since the name does not self-explain the finance or couples use case without the subtitle context.

**Icon Concept:**

A clean, rounded-square icon in deep indigo or slate blue with a single soft white chevron-arrow curving gently upward-right — suggesting forward momentum and a gentle push without urgency. The arrow is thick and rounded, not sharp, reinforcing the calm coaching personality. Optional: a very subtle two-tone gradient (indigo to cobalt) to signal premium iOS design quality. No text on the icon. The shape reads instantly at small sizes (widget, Watch complication).

## Icon Design Brief

This brief is for the Designer. The icon must be production-ready for all required App Store sizes and must read clearly at the smallest required display size (Watch complication, 20x20pt).

### Concept

A single abstracted flame or ember shape rendered as a clean upward-pointing rounded geometric form. The shape should be simultaneously readable as:

- A flame (warmth, energy, life)
- A house roofline (home, shelter, partnership)

The dual reading is intentional and should feel natural, not forced. Do not attempt to combine both motifs explicitly — the abstraction does the work.

### Color Palette

| Mode | Foreground (shape) | Background |
|---|---|---|
| Light | Deep terracotta or burnt sienna | Warm off-white or cream |
| Dark | Warm coral | Deep charcoal |

Both palettes must be submitted. The light mode version is primary for App Store listing screenshots.

### Typography

No text on the icon under any circumstances.

### Style Constraints

- iOS-native rounded square container (no custom shape)
- No drop shadows
- No gradients unless extremely subtle (avoid the "AI slop" look)
- No literal fire imagery — the shape is abstract and geometric
- No partnership/couple iconography (two figures, two flames, etc.) — the warmth is implied by the palette and form, not depicted
- Minimum detail: the icon must be legible and identifiable at 20x20pt

### Reference Aesthetic

Copilot (finance app) — restraint, premium, instantly readable. Apply the same geometric discipline but with a warmer, more intimate palette. The goal is to read "finance app that cares" not "fintech startup."

### Deliverables Required

- 1024x1024px master (PNG, no alpha, RGB)
- All App Store required sizes exported from master
- Dark mode variant at 1024x1024px
- Figma source file with all artboards

### Known Issues (Non-Blocking)

1. **Zero intrinsic finance keyword signal in the name.** Hearth carries no finance keyword in the name itself — 100% of ASO search weight must come from the subtitle and keyword field. This leaves no redundancy if subtitle optimization changes in a future App Store metadata update. Mitigated by a strong subtitle and disciplined keyword field management.

2. **Minor namespace collision with Hearth.com.** Hearth.com is a home improvement financing platform (B2B/home services). The App Store Finance category is clean and the target audiences are entirely non-overlapping. Risk is non-blocking but should be monitored in PR coverage and app review contexts.

3. **Two of three agents proposed the same name.** This limits diversity of comparison but reflects genuine independent convergence rather than process bias. The committee's selection process evaluated the proposals on their merits.

4. **Slight archaic register of "hearth" for younger Gen Z users.** The fireplace connotation may require brief onboarding orientation for users unfamiliar with the term. Mitigated by the icon (visual warmth cue) and the tagline ("Where your money comes home" is immediately legible).

### QA Notes

The committee's decision is sound and well-documented. The final subtitle "AI Finance for Couples" at 22 characters represents an improvement over Agent B's original "AI Finance for Two" — "Couples" is a higher-intent, more specific keyword than "Two". The scoring methodology is consistent across all proposals with five dimensions applied uniformly. The rationale for selecting Hearth over Nudge is defensible across emotional fit, brand scalability, and word-of-mouth mechanics. No blocking issues identified.
